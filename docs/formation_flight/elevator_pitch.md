# Elevator Pitch

A fleet of 4–6 small solar-powered aircraft flying in formation at
65,000 feet with indefinite endurance — months, not hours. They
self-deploy from the US mainland to the Western Pacific in under a week
with no runway, no fuel, no forward base, and no logistics tail. By day
they spread out independently for wide-area ISR coverage; at night they
form up and surf each other's wake vortices to survive on batteries until
dawn. Each aircraft costs under $300K at volume — 50× cheaper than a
Global Hawk — and losing one doesn't kill the mission. They carry
sensors, comms relays, PNT beacons, or deployable drone swarms, and
they're nearly impossible to shoot down: small composite airframes with
no radar signature, no heat signature, spread across the sky at an
altitude most air defenses can't reach. This is persistent,
distributed, attritable airborne infrastructure — a cell tower, a GPS
satellite, and an arsenal plane that runs on sunlight and never lands.
